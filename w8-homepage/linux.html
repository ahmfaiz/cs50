I came across Linux online in 2021, and after learning about it for a couple of months, I decided to give it a try.

And I haven't looked back since.

Linux at its core is very basic and not that user-friendly. Hence, people have built distributions (customized versions) around it.

The first distro I tried was Zorin due to its similarity to Windows (which it does best), but on tinkering with its customizations, I found that I didn't miss windows that much. So, about 2 months in, I switched to Fedora and stayed here since.

At first the command line, terminal, shell, desktop environment, bash, etc. all seemed a bit daunting, but now, for many of my use cases, I prefer cli-tools over GUI ones :)

I would recommend Linux to anyone who wants to learn how computers work under the hood; it is a must-try for anyone in CS.
